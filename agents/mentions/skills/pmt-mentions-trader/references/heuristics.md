# Heuristics

## Entry / pricing
- Price matters more than the binary outcome.
- Do not chase a strike after it already steamed past your original edge.
- Use neighboring and correlated markets to estimate fair value.
- In some live spots, taking aggressively is better than missing the move while waiting for passive fills.
- As the market gets sharper, edge often comes from one extra layer of context beyond what the crowd already checked.
- If the market is pricing off pooled historicals, setup shorthand, or generic transcript frequency, rebuild the comparable set before trusting the number.
- In named events, direct-name paths often deserve higher probability than a loose basket of theme-adjacent words.

## Execution
- Limit orders are alpha in mention markets.
- Scale in with smaller layered orders when books are thin.
- For small portfolios, simple microstructure edge can outperform overconfident prediction.
- Prefer maker-first execution by default, but switch aggressive if the edge is clearly decaying faster than passive fills are arriving.
- Treat orderbook behavior itself as information: full-size fills, sudden book wipes, or a price running away can be live tells, not just noise.
- Do not copy-trade after the move unless you can independently rebuild the thesis at the new number.

## Timing / setup
- Reprice immediately when the event frame changes.
- Wait as late as practical when timing itself gives edge.
- Venue, format, and Q&A setup are predictive features.
- As the event goes live, liquidity dries up and stale orders become dangerous.
- Partial trigger progression can create temporary edge, but dispute risk rises too.
- Setup labels like open press / closed press are inputs, not destiny.
- Some words are late-path words. “Not said yet” is not automatically “dead.”

## Modeling / uncertainty
- Rare formats should be priced with wider uncertainty bands.
- Historical comparables are useful only after adjusting for the current narrative regime.
- If event-specific context is weak, reduce size instead of pretending confidence.
- Recurring markets get solved by segmentation, not raw averages: event type, announcer, network, quarter, and context all can matter.
- Thin-history speakers and weird one-offs should carry explicit uncertainty discounts.
- Model prepared remarks and Q&A separately when the word path materially changes between phases.

## Portfolio / capital allocation
- Bond-style markets are worthwhile only when the gap from 90-95 to true 99-ish probability is still meaningful.
- Separate compounding / low-risk capital from high-ROI mention-market capital.
- Capital allocation matters: a profitable bond-like trade can still be inferior if it crowds out faster recurring edge.
- If realized edge depends heavily on fill quality, size for actual execution quality, not just paper fair value.
- Chronic undersizing becomes a major leak once your edge is proven and your bankroll has grown.
