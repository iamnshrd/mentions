# Examples

## Example 1 — Iran press conference
- **Event:** Trump press conference focused on Iran
- **Main narrative:** Iran escalation, military posture, diplomacy / demands
- **Fresh changes:** any strike/reprisal update, venue or format confirmation, Q&A confirmation
- **Direct paths:** Iran, nuclear, ceasefire, deal, Supreme Leader, Khamenei-type phrasing
- **Weak paths:** unrelated domestic political names unless Trump drifts hard
- **Q&A watch:** high relevance if reporters are likely to ask broader geopolitical follow-ups
- **Probably priced in:** broad Iran/oil/hormuz heat
- **Unknowns:** how narrow or wide Trump keeps the framing

## Example 2 — White House press briefing
- **Event:** recurring briefing with spokesperson
- **Main narrative:** agenda topics from the last 24-48h
- **Fresh changes:** breaking White House news, policy shifts, legal rulings, foreign-policy escalations
- **Direct paths:** topics already central to briefing prep
- **Weak paths:** random slogan markets without a current narrative hook
- **Q&A watch:** very important because late-question topics can dominate mention paths
- **Probably priced in:** obvious headliner topics that have already moved all morning
- **Unknowns:** whether the briefing becomes defensive, celebratory, or reactive

## Example 3 — Earnings call mention market
- **Event:** prepared remarks + analyst Q&A
- **Main narrative:** earnings, guidance, margins, AI/product themes, whatever is dominating the company setup
- **Fresh changes:** pre-call headlines, regulatory news, major product launches, prior guidance changes
- **Direct paths:** topics management must address or analysts are likely to press on
- **Weak paths:** buzzwords with no obvious business path
- **Q&A watch:** often decisive for late-word markets
- **Probably priced in:** house phrases and obvious call themes
- **Unknowns:** whether the key word comes from prepared remarks, management follow-up, or analyst questions
