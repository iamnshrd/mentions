# Pricing Signals

Use this file when the market looks wrong, but you need to say *how* it is wrong.

Pricing signals are not the same as outcomes. They are clues about what the crowd is mis-weighting.

## 1. Pooled historical rate is lying
What it means:
- market is using one broad historical bucket
- the real comparable set is narrower

Common clues:
- all-games average cited for a prime-time-only product
- no distinction by network / booth / event type

Typical action:
- rebuild fair value on the correctly segmented subset

## 2. Setup-label overreaction
What it means:
- market moved too far off open press / closed press / room setup shorthand

Common clues:
- strong move before live event evidence
- traders acting as if setup label alone determines the whole path

Typical action:
- discount the shorthand and rebuild from actual event tree

## 3. Phase-blind pricing
What it means:
- market prices the event as one blob instead of prepared remarks vs Q&A vs late detour windows

Common clues:
- contract gets killed just because it was unsaid in opening remarks
- late-path words get treated as dead too early

Typical action:
- split the event into phases and reprice only the words actually affected

## 4. Thin-history fake precision
What it means:
- market assigns a tight number where comparables are sparse or weak

Common clues:
- rare speaker
- weird format
- tiny sample treated like a law of nature

Typical action:
- widen the range and reduce confidence / size

## 5. Direct-name path underpriced
What it means:
- a directly involved name is being priced too close to generic related concepts

Common clues:
- named counterparty / company / actor is central to event setup
- market still prices vague theme words similarly

Typical action:
- upgrade the direct name path and downgrade the loose basket

## 6. Copy-trader premium
What it means:
- current price includes follower demand, not just genuine fair-value repricing

Common clues:
- position got public attention
- move happened fast without equivalent new information

Typical action:
- do not inherit a sharp’s old trade at your new worse price unless you independently rebuild it

## 7. Field negative inference after informed buying
What it means:
- a few names in a field got pumped in a way that should weaken the rest of the field

Common clues:
- concentrated informed-looking buying
- untouched names did not reprice enough

Typical action:
- look at the rest of the field, not only the pumped contracts

## 8. Small-account spread harvest
What it means:
- edge comes from stale quotes / wide spreads, not deep forecasting

Common clues:
- tiny fills available at obviously dumb prices
- larger players likely cannot deploy size efficiently there

Typical action:
- harvest the microstructure edge instead of pretending this is a grand prediction thesis

## 9. Maker quality beats theoretical EV
What it means:
- your paper fair value is fine, but your realized edge depends mostly on whether you can actually get the fills

Common clues:
- market runs away while you wait
- queue quality matters a lot

Typical action:
- adjust for execution reality, not only model output

## 10. Bond capital lockup tradeoff
What it means:
- a decent bond-like trade may still be inferior if it ties up bankroll needed for faster recurring edge

Common clues:
- low-volatility trade with modest return
- simultaneous high-ROI recurring opportunities exist

Typical action:
- compare isolated EV with opportunity cost and bankroll flexibility
