# Anti-patterns

- Chasing a strike after it has already steamed far beyond the original edge.
- Copying someone else's position without matching their entry price or timing.
- Using blind market buys / dollar orders in thin, wide-spread event markets.
- Leaving stale resting orders up right as the live event begins.
- Treating partial trigger or almost-event as if it were already cleanly resolved.
- Assuming a strike is bad just because it sounds unlikely, without checking price.
- Modeling a strike in isolation when its real path depends on venue, Q&A, or event format.
- Forcing fake precision in rare or weakly-modeled formats.
- Copy-pasting historical mention frequencies without checking whether the current narrative regime changed.
- Treating open press / closed press shorthand as the whole model instead of one setup variable.
- Killing late-path / Q&A-dependent words too early just because they missed the opening phase.
- Treating field names independently after informed flow already repriced the rest of the field.
- Ignoring capital allocation and tying up bankroll in mediocre bond-like trades while better recurring edge exists.
- Sizing off paper fair value without discounting for bad fills, slow entry, or poor execution quality.
